Zero Emission autonomous thrusters

Tow-botic Systems is developing zero emission underwater robots (Towbots) to autonomously attach thrusters directly to the ship's hull upon arrival in port. The ship can then be controlled from the bridge by the Master or Pilot using a handheld device, or autonomously maneuvered to the desired wharf.

Benefits of our technology

  • Response time improved by 98% (Push to pull 2-3 secs) – Instantly applied thrust
  • Direct control of thrusters - No dependence on the tug captain, dynamic positioning possible either from the ship’s bridge or autonomously
  • Battery driven - Zero emissions, No fuel spillage risks
  • 24-hour operation - Fewer operational headaches and no lost productivity, No drydocking
  • Safety - No crew injury during handling of towing lines, No tug girding
  • Eliminate inefficiencies – Tugs idle 50%-60% of operating time
  • Reduces operating cost by 50% per tug annually
  • Flexibility - Full thrust available in heavy seas, ship control inside narrow locks

Available autonomous thruster configurations

  • Optional sizes: 60 kw, 150 kw, 300 kw, 450 kw, 600 kw
  • Optional configurations: Non-azimuth thrusters for athwartship thrust only, 360 deg azimuth thrusters extend below keel, 360 deg deflector do not extend below keel for shallow water operations
  • Electric energy storage system: Sized as per specific port and project requirements


Quick connect detachable thrusters

Easy to install portable thrusters for barges, pontoons, jack-ups, transhippers and shuttle vessels. Portable dynamic positioning system to convert a vessel at very short notice for offshore support and marine construction activities.The portable thrusters are able to reach to the keel of any vessel, irrespective of deep draft or high freeboard.

Benefits of our technology

  • Reduced mobilization & demobilization costs
  • Reduced operational time lost for conversion
  • Can be easily inspected and cleaned of propeller fouling by lifting out of the water
  • Lower maintenance cost by stowage on deck inside closed container when not in use
  • Lower capital cost by having fewer thruster equipment that can be quickly interchanged between barges
  • Power supply can be varied as per project requirements (barge power source or mobile sources such as diesel electric, electric and fuel cells to lower emissions)

Available detachable thruster configurations

  • Optional sizes: 60 kw, 150 kw, 300 kw, 450 kw, 600 kw
  • Optional configurations:Non-azimuth thrusters for athwartship thrust only, 360 deg azimuth thrusters extend below keel, 360 deg deflector do not extend below keel for shallow water operations
  • Mobile power source: Type and size as per specific project requirements

Energy efficient underwater magnets

Deep water magnetic attachments for subsea attaching and lifting operations. Specially designed permanent magnet arrays require no energy when attached. Suitable for divers to quickly attach to a work surface. Can be used for station keeping of ROVs during inspection & maintenance, saving energy while attached.

Benefits of our technology

  • Magnets can be easily attached together to create any shape/size/curvature depending on the task
  • Holding capability over a large air gap
  • Fenders enable gentle handling of painted surfaces
  • Feedback of holding force on the attach surface available
  • Untethered operations possible using power from onboard energy cells for ‘switch-on/off’

Available magnets

  • Holding force of each magnet: 0.5T, 1T and 2T (multiple magnets can be combined in any configuration)
  • Single power pack for all magnets or individual power pack for each magnet

Digital Twin Of Maritime Operations Using Discrete Event Simulations

Tow-botic Systems develops discrete event simulation models for optimization of port operations, predicting shipping emissions and operating costs in port and approaches. These models can be used as a digital twin of your port to run different scenarios and assess the impacts of planned changes.

Benefits of our technology

  • Identify bottlenecks and assist in optimum planning of the infrastructure
  • Visualize a process and explore the potential effects of modifications before actual implementation
  • Estimate shipping emissions in port and approaches at every instant, and plan an emission reduction strategy for your port
  • Determine the infrastructure upgrades that have a large impact on operating costs
  • Use live AIS data to continuously update vessel positions & re-run the simulations

Optimizing port operations

We have completed numerous port and shipping optimization projects. Our experience includes port master-planning, market demand assessments, equipment planning and utilization assessment, navigation channels, ship manoeuvring simulations, dredge optimization, estimation of capital and operating costs, risk assessments, recommending tariffs for project viability and due diligence for investment in marine projects.


We have also completed many coastal and transhipment solutions, selection of optimum vessel sizes, transportation cost assessment, planning & risk assessments for bulk handling ports and supply chain infrastructure from mine to port for various mines in Indonesia, Brazil, Mali, Mauritania and Guinea.


Our understanding of port operations as well as vessel operations gives us an added advantage when advising and recommending solutions specifically suited to your project constraints.
